Part 2: Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)

Why do some members stay while others disappear?

This section highlights key behavioral patterns and member characteristics by analyzing tenure, engagement, and referral source, revealing early signs of churn risk.

View full notebook on GitHub

TL;DR
  • Context: exploratory analysis to understand churn patterns in a dataset of 2,081 members across 9 studios.
  • Role: led EDA and data quality checks; framed hypotheses and feature candidates for modeling.
  • Approach: sanity checks (duplicates, missingness, outliers); class balance and churn rate; segment cuts by tenure, engagement, referral source, and region; leakage checks; correlation/collinearity review; time trends/cohorts.
  • Result: engagement and tenure emerged as primary drivers; younger cohorts churn more; standardized categories and produced a clean, model-ready feature set.
  • Tech: Python (pandas, matplotlib/plotly), Jupyter.

EDA Highlights

Studio Locations & Churn Trends

Studio addresses were geocoded to visualize coverage across metro Denver, helping pinpoint regional churn trends and location-driven performance differences. Churn rates across locations range from 29% to 38%, underscoring the importance of location-specific retention strategies.

Studio Member Volume and Churn Rate Takeaways
  • High-risk studios: Highlands Ranch (38.4%), Hilltop Village (37.9%), and Castle Rock (36.7%) show the highest churn, pointing to potential engagement or operational issues.
  • Top performer: Lowry leads with the lowest churn (29.2%), suggesting strong onboarding or retention practices.
  • Studios like Uptown Denver and Applewood Village (mid-30% churn) are stable but offer room for optimization.
Demographic Insights by Location

Understanding member demographics across locations is crucial for tailored retention.

  • Age Distribution: Age often serves as a proxy for stability and preferences. Certain studios skew older (e.g., Broomfield), which may correlate with lower churn or different class preferences.
  • Gender Mix: Every studio maintains a relatively balanced gender mix (roughly 30-40% Female, Male, and Non-binary representation), reflecting an inclusive environment and presenting opportunities for personalized engagement.
    • Female-Leaning Studios: Applewood Village (42.5%) and Hilltop Village (38.6%) have the highest share of Female members, potentially signaling a brand position that resonates with this demographic.
    • Male-Leaning Studios: Castle Rock (36.7%) and Lowry (36.6%) have slightly higher Male representation, suggesting differences in class demand or referral patterns, which can inform staffing and marketing efforts.
What do loyal members have in common?

This chart reveals the relationships between member behaviors and traits. Darker colors indicate stronger correlations (blue for positive, red for negative).

  • Unexpected Finding: Auto-renew settings had almost no impact on churn. Despite common assumptions, members who manually renew aren't any more likely to leave.
  • Engagement is Key: Members with higher engagement scores are significantly less likely to churn, reinforcing that consistency builds commitment.
  • Tenure Matters: Longer-standing members show more loyalty, supporting the value of cohort-based onboarding and long-term retention strategies.

So What? Turning Exploration into Action

Each insight from this analysis directly points to specific strategies studio leadership can use to improve member retention:

  • Engagement is Key to Loyalty: Consistent attendance and diverse class participation are strong loyalty signals. Focus on keeping members active and engaged is crucial for long-term loyalty.
  • The Six-Month Mark is Critical: Churn risk drops significantly once members pass their six-month anniversary. This highlights the importance of strong onboarding and targeted retention efforts during those crucial first few months.
  • Demographics Matter by Location: Studios with younger, more transient member populations might need different approaches than those with older, more stable demographics. Tailor pricing, communication, or class offerings to fit each studio's unique member base.
