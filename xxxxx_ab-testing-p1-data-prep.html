Payment Recovery A/B Test

Updated March 2026

Does the way you ask matter as much as what you're asking for?

When a payment fails, most businesses send the same message: a transactional notice, a dollar amount, a deadline. It's efficient. It's also a missed opportunity.

This project tests the assumption that the tone of an outreach message meaningfully changes whether a member pays. Using a Bayesian A/B testing framework, it measures the recovery rate lift from empathetic vs. transactional messaging across distinct member segments, and translates those results into a phased rollout recommendation backed by quantified revenue impact.

The methodology is built to be reusable. The core business problem, optimizing outreach at a moment of financial friction, applies anywhere customers interact with billing: healthcare, fintech, SaaS, consumer lending.

Results at a Glance

  • There is a 99.9% probability that empathetic messaging outperforms transactional messaging on payment recovery rate: P(Empathetic > Transactional).
  • Expected lift: +5.2% For every 100 members contacted, empathetic messaging recovers approximately 5 additional payments compared to the transactional baseline.
  • Annualized revenue impact: $542K to $1.2M Depending on rollout scope, the switch to empathetic messaging generates between $542K and $1.2M in additional recovered revenue per year.
  • Phased rollout captures 80% of expected revenue. By deploying selectively to segments where the evidence is strongest, the phased approach recovers 80% of the full-population revenue opportunity while protecting against premature deployment where confidence is lower.

The problem with one-size-fits-all outreach

Sending the same message to a first-time missed payment and a chronic delinquent isn't just inefficient, it may actively reduce recovery rates in one group while improving them in another. This project segments the population before the test, models the treatment effect by segment, and produces a rollout recommendation that reflects the actual evidence rather than the average.

What's inside this project

Part 1 - Data Prep & Experiment Design
Cleans the population, engineers behavioral segments, and builds a statistically sound experiment with stratified assignment and balance validation

Part 2 - EDA
Examines recovery rates, balance distributions, and segment behavior before the experiment runs

Part 3 - Bayesian A/B Test
Models the posterior distribution of recovery rates for each arm, estimates P(B>A), and segments the treatment effect

Part 4 - Business Impact & Rollout Strategy
Translates posterior results into dollar-denominated revenue scenarios and a phased deployment recommendation

Measure twice, deploy in phases

The goal isn't just a statistically significant result: it's a recommendation a business leader can act on with confidence. That means knowing not just whether the treatment works, but for whom, by how much, and what it's worth.
